<p><strong>The first song I fell in&nbsp;love with</strong><br>My older sister, Penny, played me So Long, Marianne by Leonard Cohen when I was eight,&nbsp;like some kind of&nbsp;initiation, to say: “Now&nbsp;<em>this</em> is a real poet.” It felt like contraband and so different to all the pop flotsam I had heard in my otherwise white, suburban upbringing, and gave me a taste of adult romantic relationships that a child could not possibly understand. I love my sister and I wanted to impress her.</p> <p><strong>The first single I bought</strong><br>I was given WH Smith tokens as a child, so I&nbsp;must have used the bloody things. When I&nbsp;was 15, I ordered Hey&nbsp;Joe/Radio Ethiopia by Patti Smith through the post and would play&nbsp;it like it was the word of God.</p>       <figure class="element element-video" data-canonical-url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JkushEk_JCA"                                                                        >  <iframe height="480" width="854" src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/JkushEk_JCA?wmode=opaque&feature=oembed" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen ></iframe> </figure>   <p><strong>The best song to play at&nbsp;a party</strong> <br>8 Ball by Underworld is astonishing. It’s slinky, sexy and seduces you.</p> <p><strong>The song I do at karaoke</strong><br>I don’t usually do karaoke, but if the machine stretched to Paddy McGinty’s Goat by&nbsp;Val Doonican, I’d&nbsp;be&nbsp;there.</p> <p><strong>The song I no longer listen to</strong><br>I had a brief flirtation with glam at 13, and loved Sebastian by Cockney Rebel. It now sounds preposterous, full of pretentious strings and a nonsense chorus.</p>       <figure class="element element-video" data-canonical-url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A3DUsxOIZi4"                                                                        >  <iframe height="480" width="640" src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/A3DUsxOIZi4?wmode=opaque&feature=oembed" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen ></iframe> </figure>   <p><strong>The song I secretly like, but tell everybody I hate</strong><br>I guess people wouldn’t think Billie Eilish or Beyoncé are in my canon, but they are both incredible. I have a soft spot for Vincent van Gogh, so Vincent by Don McLean would move me to tears when I was younger.</p> <p><strong>The best song to have&nbsp;sex to</strong><br>I was having sex to Discreet Music by Brian Eno by 18, and have ended up associating his&nbsp;ambient music with sex rather than new age music. It’s great music to make love to because it slows everything down, which men need and, in&nbsp;my experience, women appreciate.</p> <p><strong>The song that changed my life</strong><br>I first heard Birdland by Patti Smith on the night I was told that my father would probably die, when I was 16. It taught me that music could be medicine for someone in&nbsp;pain. The lyrics are about maverick psychotherapist Wilhelm Reich, whom I&nbsp;ended up studying, and helped lead me into the world of alternative healing that has enriched my life.</p> <p><strong>The song that gets me up in the morning</strong><br>I can’t listen to music in the morning. I’m a slow riser, and so need a quiet cup of tea to gather my thoughts.</p>       <figure class="element element-video" data-canonical-url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-sEP0-8VAow"                                                                        >  <iframe height="480" width="640" src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/-sEP0-8VAow?wmode=opaque&feature=oembed" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen ></iframe> </figure>   <p><strong>The song that makes&nbsp;me&nbsp;cry</strong><br>The live version of I&nbsp;Wish I Knew How It Would Feel to Be Free by&nbsp;Nina Simone destroys me. It embodies the weight that she carried of Black oppression with her mental health. The version on YouTube is astonishing – she’s right on the edge where she can still function, which is an incredible line for any artist to walk.</p> <p><strong>The song I’d like played at my funeral</strong><br>Upon This Rock by Jocelyn Pook, which James have as our intro&nbsp;music before we go on stage.</p> <p><em>Tim Booth’s debut novel, When I Died for the First&nbsp;Time, is out now</em><em> in hardback and audiobook</em><em>. <a href="https://wearejames.com/live/">James&nbsp;tour in April</a>.</em></p>  <figure class="element element-embed" data-alt="Tim Booth’s honest playlist">  <iframe data-testid="embed-iframe" style="border-radius:12px" src="https://open.spotify.com/embed/playlist/44T484Dc3lsZCiVxd2AtQe?utm_source=generator" width="100%" height="352" frameBorder="0" allowfullscreen="" allow="autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; fullscreen; picture-in-picture" loading="lazy"></iframe> </figure>
