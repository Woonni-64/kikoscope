<p>Hello and welcome to another edition of The Crunch!</p> <p>It was very tempting to make this week’s newsletter entirely about a <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/science/artemis-ii">certain ongoing space voyage</a> you may have heard about. But there’s lots happening in the world, so we also have some charts on the <strong>strait of Hormuz</strong>, <strong>Melbourne’s seasons</strong> and more.</p> <h2>But first … is it still ‘space’ if it’s full?</h2> <p>To kick us off with a story from a <em>different</em> part of space, my UK colleagues have visualised the 32,000 objects currently orbiting Earth.</p>    <p>But perhaps even more alarming than the sheer number, is how fast this has grown – from about 7,000 objects in the 1970s and 20,000 in the 90s. The real map is animated – making it far more terrifying – and there are a bunch of other cool charts <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/science/ng-interactive/2026/mar/31/this-feels-fragile-how-a-satellite-smashing-chain-reaction-could-spiral-out-of-control?CMP=Share_iOSApp_Other">in here that you should check out</a>.</p> <p>Back in Australia, Andy Ball and I have been <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2026/apr/08/petrol-prices-fuel-tracker-crisis-check-price-diesel-gas-shortage-oil-reserves">tracking the fuel crisis</a>, including this animated map showing the (still) hundreds of service stations that are out of one or more fuel types:</p>    <p>That page is being updated regularly with the latest fuel prices across major cities, outages, shipments and the country’s fuel reserves.</p> <p>Julian Fell and Casey Briggs at the ABC are using similar data to <a href="https://www.abc.net.au/news/2026-04-01/petrol-diesel-price-tracker/106513484">estimate fuel prices by capital city and regional classification</a>. Julian also had a <a href="https://www.abc.net.au/news/2026-03-31/petrol-prices-accc-shape-market/106504912">neat story</a> about how the variation in fuel prices has reduced as margins get squeezed.</p> <h2>Four charts from the fortnight</h2> <p>***</p> <p><strong>1. But ours didn’t have any interactive bar charts. Very jealous of Reuters’ bar charts.</p> <p>***</p> <p><strong>2. That’s just too many</strong></p> <p>How many seasons does Melbourne have? As an <s>unwilling</s> Melbourne resident I’d say it has two – cold and colder. But software developer Max Craigie has built a <a href="https://seasons.lotlsoft.com/">very cool app</a> that suggests there are actually seven:</p>    <p>The <a href="https://seasons.lotlsoft.com/methodology">methodology page</a> on this app is fascinating – even using cosine similarity to match season names with weather data! Max also notes that the Wurundjeri and other Kulin Nation peoples have long recognised seven seasons in Melbourne, although this drew on cultural practice and knowledge of plants and animals, rather than just weather.</p> <p>I tried to investigate whether Melbourne’s famous “four seasons in a day” is true a few years ago, and wound up <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/datablog/2023/may/23/which-australian-city-has-the-best-weather-climate-most-sunny-days-where-should-i-live-interactive">making a quiz to help you find where to live</a>.</p> <p>***</p> <p><strong>3. A very detailed map</strong></p> <p>There’s been a lot of brilliant maps made of the strait of Hormuz, but <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2026/04/06/world/middleeast/iran-islands-strait-of-hormuz-control.html">this incredibly detailed one</a> by the New York Times really helped me get my bearings. It shows the scale of the Persian Gulf, and helps you understand where important sites like Kharg and Larak actually are.</p>    <p><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2026/04/05/world/middleeast/war-middle-east-five-maps.html">This</a> was another great map piece from the NYT, showing the scale of the war. And the ABC <a href="https://www.abc.net.au/news/2026-03-28/verify-mapping-israel-s-bombing-of-lebanese-bridges/106504560">mapped Israel’s destruction of bridges</a> in southern Lebanon.</p> <p>***</p> <p><strong>4.
