<p><strong>“In their Champions League match against PSV Eindhoven, Bayern Munich <a href="https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/match/2046040--psv-vs-bayern-munchen/">made four substitutions</a> in the 62nd minute,</strong>” writes Stephan Wijnen. <strong>“The four players entering the pitch together had a combined estimated value of €265m (Harry Kane, Michael Olise, Serge Gnabry and Alphonso Davies). Is this the most expensive combined substitution ever?”</strong></p> <p>Before we go any further – a player’s estimated value is not an objective measure, but using transfer fees doesn’t necessarily work, with some players moving for no fee (Kylian Mbappé, for example). Like Stephan in his question, we are going to use <a href="https://www.transfermarkt.co.uk/spieler-statistik/wertvollstespieler/marktwertetop">Transfermarkt’s valuations</a> in a bid for consistency, and will focus on the value of players coming on.</p> <p>Let’s start with Bayern, who matched their Champions League effort in November’s Bundesliga win over Leverkusen as Kane (€65m), Olise (€130m) and Luis Díaz (€70m) came on together in the 59th minute – with their team already 3-0 up.</p> <p>Mykola Kozlenko has three suggestions, and it may not surprise you to learn that two of them come from Real Madrid. “In January 2025, Real brought on four players after 63 minutes in the Copa del Rey against Minera – Vinícius Júnior (€200m), Mbappé (€160m), Lucas Vázquez (€3.5m) and Chema Andrés (€500,000).”</p> <p>That’s a total of €370m (£322.5m) based on market values at the time – but Madrid can beat that by €10m (£8.7m) with just two simultaneous substitutes. “In the 62nd minute of their <a href="https://www.espn.co.uk/football/commentary/_/gameId/704966">La Liga game at Alavés</a> last April, Vinícius Júnior (€200m) and Jude Bellingham (€180m) came on for Rodrygo and Arda Güler,” notes Mykola.</p>    <p>We’ve had a look around for recent examples – largely Euro megaclubs in Cup competitions – but can’t top Madrid’s total. In Manchester City’s recent Carabao Cup semi-final, second leg against Newcastle, Pep Guardiola sent on Erling Haaland (€200m), Rayan Cherki (€50m) and Rodri (€75m) together in the 71st minute – a total of €325m (£283.3m).</p> <p>Elsewhere, Barcelona brought on Raphinha (€80m), Pedri (€140m) and Robert Lewandowski (€9m) together during last month’s Copa del Rey tie against Racing Santander (total: €229m), while PSG threw on Désiré Doue (€90m), Nuno Mendes (€75m) and Ousmane Dembélé (€100m) in their Coupe de France clash with Paris FC. A total of €265m, but it didn’t help much – Luis Enrique’s side <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/football/2026/jan/14/coupe-de-france-magic-paris-fc-beat-psg-favourites-holders-underdogs">lost the game 1-0</a>.</p> <p>Jürgen Klopp’s <a href="https://www.espn.co.uk/football/commentary/_/gameId/671391">quadruple Liverpool substitution</a> at Aston Villa in May 2024 didn’t pay off either. With his team leading 3-1, Klopp sent on Darwin Núñez (€70m), Dominik Szoboszlai (€75m), Ryan Gravenberch (€35m) and Curtis Jones (€35m) – €215m (£187.4m) in new blood, but Villa grabbed a draw thanks to two late Jhon Durán goals.</p> <p>How about international teams? Well, one example can be found a few miles from Knowledge HQ, at Wembley Stadium. “<a href="https://www.google.com/search?q=england+v+serbia&amp;oq=england+v+serbia&amp;gs_lcrp=EgZjaHJvbWUyCQgAEEUYORiABDIHCAEQABiABDIHCAIQABiABDIHCAMQABiABDIHCAQQABiABDIHCAUQABiABDIHCAYQABiABDIHCAcQABiABDIHCAgQABiABDIHCAkQABiABNIBCDI4NTRqMGo5qAIGsAIB8QWhD9DxWs2FBA&amp;sourceid=chrome&amp;ie=UTF-8#sie=m;/g/11lyp24_tb;2;/g/11f766qmsx;tl;fp;1;;;;-1">England v Serbia in November 2025</a>, 64th minute: Bellingham (€180m), Phil Foden (€80m), Eberechi Eze (€65m) and Jordan Henderson (€2.5m) all came on together – a total of €327.5m (£285.4m),” adds Mykola.</p> <ul> <li><p>Can you do any better? <a href="mailto:knowledge@theguardian.com">Email us</a> with your answers</p></li> </ul> <h2><strong>Perfect hat-trick of assists</strong></h2> <p><strong>“In Liverpool’s 6-0 win over Qarabag, Virgil van Dijk achieved a ‘perfect’ hat trick of assists (one with his right foot, one with his left foot, one with his head),”</strong> writes Joe Adamson. <strong>“How common is it for a player to manage this in one game?”</strong></p> <p>Another Liverpool player can match Van Dijk’s achievement, with an added twist. “Against Arsenal in 1995, Stan Collymore provided <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gOOzZr3HNwo">a perfect hat-trick of assists</a> for Robbie Fowler to score a perfect hat-trick. That’s <strong>50 games</strong> without a penalty. Incidentally, Brian Deane scored both.”</p> <p>At Italia 90, England ended a remarkable <strong>53-game, </strong>four-and-a-half year run without being awarded a penalty. Bryan Robson scored one in Israel on 26 February 1986, and that was it until the quarter-final against Cameroon on 1 July 1990. It was Gary Lineker’s first penalty for England in his 56th game; he scored it to equalise with seven minutes remaining, then netted another penalty in extra-time.</p> <p>Chris Rawson wrote in to say that Burnley are currently on a run of 47 games without a penalty (the Clarets ended up with five spot-kicks that season), while Sean DeLoughry had an even better example. “Galway United can push the record up to <a href="https://connachttribune.ie/murrays-late-penalty-earns-united-share-spoils-444/">an impressive </a><strong><a href="https://connachttribune.ie/murrays-late-penalty-earns-united-share-spoils-444/">72 games</a></strong>.”</p> <p>Now, as David points out, the U’s have surpassed that. “I think the total is now 84 games after the 0-0 draw at Coventry – none since the second leg of our League One playoff semi-final.” Cameron Brannagan (who once scored four penalties in one game) scored his spot-kick against Peterborough 635 days ago, and one Oxford fan is <a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c07jnpv2r77o">vowing not to shave</a> until his side get another penalty.</p>    <p>Oxford’s run is now at 85 games after Tuesday’s home defeat by Norwich – level with Mansfield Town, who endured what is thought to be a Football League record penalty drought between 30 November 2013 and 22 August 2015.
