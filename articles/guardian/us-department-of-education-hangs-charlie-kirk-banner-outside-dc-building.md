<p>The US Department of Education has hung large banners outside its building in Washington DC, including one featuring an image of the late far-right commentator, Charlie Kirk.</p> <p>Kirk, who was <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/sep/10/charlie-kirk-shot-utah">shot and killed last September</a> while speaking at a campus event a Utah Valley University, co-founded the conservative non-profit organization <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/ng-interactive/2024/jun/25/charlie-kirk-turning-point-usa-chase-the-vote-trump">Turning Point USA</a>, which advocates for and promotes conservative politics among young people, particularly on college campuses.</p> <p>Photos show that Kirk’s image has been displayed on the Department of Education building alongside banners honoring <a href="https://www.cwhf.org/inductees/catharine-beecher">Catharine Beecher, </a>a prominent 19th-century US educator and advocate for women’s education, and Booker T Washington, an influential educator and writer who championed <a href="https://nmaahc.si.edu/explore/stories/booker-t-washington-and-atlanta-compromise">educational opportunities</a> for Black Americans in post-civil war America and became the first principal of Tuskegee Normal and Industrial School in Alabama, now Tuskegee University.</p> <p>A banner next to the three figures reads: “Empowering our States to tell the Stories of our Heroes in American Education.”</p> <aside class="element element-rich-link element--thumbnail"> <p> <span>Related: </span><a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/ng-interactive/2026/feb/17/people-fired-punished-posting-charlie-kirk-death">More than 600 people were fired or punished for posting about Charlie Kirk’s death. They want justice</a> </p> </aside>  <p>The Department of Education said in a statement: “We are proud to honor visionary leaders whose contributions have shaped the future of education for generations.</p> <p>“Their work reflects Benjamin Franklin’s timeless belief that ‘an investment in knowledge pays the best interest,” she said, adding: “As our country marks a historic 250th milestone, this moment invites us all to join in the pursuit of fostering educational opportunity that empowers every learner to rise, contribute and help shape a brighter future for generations yet to come.”</p> <p>The banner featuring Kirk has sparked criticism as the commentator was a <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/sep/11/charlie-kirk-quotes-beliefs">polarizing figure</a> who had made incendiary and often <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/sep/11/charlie-kirk-quotes-beliefs">racist and sexist comments</a> during his career. Some users online also noted that Kirk <a href="https://www.facebook.com/realCharlieKirk/posts/abolish-the-department-of-education/1271298867686698/">had also called for</a> abolishing the Department of Education as recently as last year.</p> <p>Despite once calling the passage of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 a “<a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/sep/10/who-is-charlie-kirk-profile">mistake</a>”, describing abortion as <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2025/09/27/us/politics/charlie-kirk-debate.html">“worse”</a> than the Holocaust, and <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/sep/11/charlie-kirk-quotes-beliefs">arguing that</a> “it’s worth it to have a cost of, unfortunately, some gun deaths every single year so that we can have the second amendment to protect our other God-given rights”, Donald Trump and his allies have <a href="https://www.foxcarolina.com/2025/09/21/trump-supporters-maga-movement-honor-charlie-kirk-memorial-service/">praised Kirk</a> as a “great American hero” and a “martyr” for freedom. In October, Trump <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/oct/14/charlie-kirk-presidential-medal-of-freedom-trump">posthumously awarded</a> Kirk the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the nation’s highest civilian award.</p> <p>The new banners follow the <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/feb/19/trump-banner-justice-department">unveiling</a> last month on the Department of Justice headquarters of a large banner of Trump’s face.</p>
