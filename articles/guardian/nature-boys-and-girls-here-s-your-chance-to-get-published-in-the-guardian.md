<p><strong>**This callout is now closed for entries – but keep hold of this link, it will reopen on Monday 1st June, when we will be asking for summer entries**</strong></p> <p>Once again, the <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/environment/series/young-country-diary">Young Country Diary series</a> is open for submissions! Every three months we ask you to send us an article written by a child aged 8-14.</p> <p>The article needs to be about a <strong>recent encounter they’ve had with nature</strong> – whether it’s a marauding toad, a fascinating flower or a garden bird.</p> <p>Crucially, it doesn’t matter if the child is a nature expert or not – we are especially keen to reach teachers who might like to get their class outside and noticing nature. Note that any child who has their article published will be paid!</p> <p><strong>The deadline for spring submissions is noon on Tuesday 5 May.</strong></p> <p>Four of the entries will be chosen to be published in the newspaper and online – two in April and two in May. (Note that any early birds who submit a piece before the 20th stand a chance of being published in April.)</p>    <h2><strong>How your child can take part</strong></h2> <p><strong>First of all, we’re looking for children age 8 to 14, so we will need your permission, as parent or guardian. Then here’s what the child needs to do:</strong></p> <p><strong>Step 1</strong> – Go out one day to where there is some nature. It could be their local woods or beach, their garden, a farm, or simply the nearest park.</p> <p><strong>Step 2</strong> – Write an article of <strong>200-250 words</strong> telling us <strong>what they saw and what happened</strong>.</p> <p><strong>Step 3</strong> – Send the article to us using the form below. We ask that you fill in the form yourself rather than the child.</p> <p><strong>Photos/drawings </strong>– These are helpful (especially landscape rather than portrait) but they are not a condition of entry. If you have some, please tell us in the “more information” field, as there’s no way to attach them to the form.</p> <h2>Hints and tips for any budding YCD writers</h2> <p>If the child gets stuck and doesn’t know what to write about, here are a few pointers:</p> <p>• Good nature writing starts with the senses – so what did you see and hear? What about smell and touch? Take notes when you’re out and about so you don’t forget</p> <p>• How would you describe what happened? Did it remind you of anything?</p> <p>• It’s great to look up some extra information about what you saw, and tell us about that too. For example, if you find an interesting-looking bug, see if you can find out what type of butterfly it was, how long they live – any juicy details!</p> <p>• You can think big, and describe the whole scene – the sky, the horizon, the landscape. Or you can think small, and tell us some details about the butterfly’s wings or the beetle’s markings.</p> <p>• What did your encounter with nature make you think about? How did it make you feel?</p>    <p>The deadline for entry is noon on <strong>Tuesday 5 May</strong>. Anyone can enter their child who is <strong>aged between 8 and 14 and based in the UK</strong>. You the parent/guardian will be contacted if your child’s piece is selected for publication, and you will be paid on behalf of the child.</p> <p>For further inspiration, here are a few recent Young Country Diaries:<br>• <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/mar/28/country-diary-a-weary-wheatear-shows-off-his-best-feature">Henry, 14, on a weary wheatear</a><br>• <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/feb/28/young-country-diary-a-new-find-for-my-collection-a-fox-skull">Jasmine, 9, on finding a fox skull</a><br>• <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/jan/24/young-country-diary-a-snowy-census-of-all-the-local-animals">Rosie, 10, on bird footprints</a></p> <p>Good luck!</p> <p>Paul, editor of Young Country Diary and Country Diary</p> <p>We ask that the parent/guardian fills in the form below, rather than your child. Your responses are secure as the form is encrypted and only the Guardian has access to your contributions. <strong>One of our journalists will be in contact with you, as parent or guardian, before we publish, so please do leave contact details.</strong></p> <h2><strong>This callout is closed.</strong></h2> <p><em><strong>If you’re having trouble using the form, click <a href="https://guardiannewsandmedia.formstack.com/forms/young_country_diary_spring">here</a>.
