<p>French media were in thrall to a victory hailed as a milestone performance, calling Paris Saint-Germain’s 100th win in the Champions League “one of the finest” in the club’s history.</p> <p>Bayern Munich will be hoping for a repeat of their goal bonanza when they host the holders in the semi-final return leg next Wednesday, with Tuesday’s <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/football/2026/apr/28/psg-bayern-munich-champions-league-semi-final-first-leg-match-report">5-4 loss in Paris</a> breaking the competition record for most goals in a last-four game.</p> <aside class="element element-rich-link element--thumbnail"> <p> <span>Related: </span><a href="https://www.theguardian.com/football/2026/apr/28/psg-bayern-munich-champions-league-semi-final-first-leg-match-report">PSG edge breathless 5-4 classic as Bayern Munich rally after Dembélé’s double</a> </p> </aside>  <p>L’Équipe described the performance as “a <a href="https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Le-psg-bat-le-bayern-dans-un-match-somptueux-a-9-buts-en-demi-finales-aller-de-la-ligue-des-champions/1671720">prodigious battle of astonishing intensity</a> and technical quality”, highlighting the performances of Khvicha Kvaratskhelia and Ousmane Dembélé, who both scored twice. Its football commentator Vincent Duluc called the game a “<a href="https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Article/La-folie-des-grandes-heures-l-analyse-de-vincent-duluc-apres-la-victoire-du-psg-contre-le-bayern-au-terme-d-un-match-epoustouflant/1671761">whirlwind of every emotion</a> and every impulse, contradictory and furious” and said it had “showcased the best of modern football”.</p> <p>Le Parisien<strong> </strong>was similarly lavish in its praise, calling the encounter “pure madness, this <a href="https://www.leparisien.fr/sports/football/ligue-des-champions/psg-bayern-munich-5-4-apres-une-demie-de-legende-paris-peut-toujours-rever-en-grand-28-04-2026-7UJ6SXWILZH6FMXOFTTBNW6JGU.php">pinnacle of art and culture</a>, this museum of world wonders, this legend for the history books – all of this must be passed on, recounted, and recounted again”.</p> <p>But it struck a cautionary note given Bayern’s late surge that could well work to their favour in Munich: “Neither side will want to back down, and the outcome will once again hinge on the sheer force of their attacks, both of which are incredibly potent.”</p> <p>In Germany, the press homed in on Bayern’s defensive vulnerabilities but remained sanguine about the return after the visitors dug deep to cut the deficit from 5-2 with two second-half goals.</p>    <p>Kicker called it a “football festival at the Parc des Princes”, a “spectacular encounter” where Bayern were nearly overwhelmed but “showed resilience” and “impressive character” to claw back to 5-4 through goals by Dayot Upamecano and Luis Díaz. “Both teams revealed their quality, but also their vulnerabilities,” Kicker reported.</p> <p>Süddeutsche Zeitung noted the absence of Bayern’s coach, Vincent Kompany, from the touchline – due to suspension – but highlighted his post-match sentiment that “the belief is 100% there” for the return. Bild<strong> </strong>concentrated on the rarity of the scoreline, calling it a “wild, wide-open football battle”.</p> <p>Italian media were in thrall to Kvaratskhelia, with La Gazzetta dello Sport<strong> </strong>lauding “Kvara” as the standout player, describing his curling finish to level the scores at 1-1 as a “moment of pure magic”. It called the match a “total spectacle. Real football, pure football. <a href="https://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Champions-League/28-04-2026/psg-bayern-monaco-live-risultato-e-gol-in-diretta-champions-league.shtml">Football in its finest essence.</a>”</p> <p>Corriere dello Sport<strong> </strong>hailed the game as “football at its absolute peak” but was circumspect about the pell-mell nature of Bayern’s performance, saying it “<a href="https://www.corrieredellosport.it/news/calcio/champions-league/2026/04/29-148290448/chiedete_ai_portieri_se_sono_felici_i_dubbi_di_seedorf_dopo_psg-bayern_il_confronto_con_milan-juve_spietato">splits the world in two</a>: on one side, there are those who saw adrenaline, senseless play, a frenetic pace … and those who saw only a game acceptable only on PlayStation”.</p> <p>In Spain, attention was heaped on the two figures linked with Barcelona, Luis Enrique – who said it was “<a href="https://www.theguardian.com/football/2026/apr/29/champions-league-paris-saint-germain-bayern-munich-luis-enrique-vincent-kompany">the best match I have ever coached</a>” – and Dembélé. Marca<strong> </strong>praised “a breathtaking 5-4 contest that felt more like an exhibition of attacking brilliance than a battle for a place in the final”. AS<strong> </strong>flagged the “non-stop drama” and praised Bayern’s “winning mentality and looking ambitiously towards the return leg”.</p>
