<p>A 19-year-old man has died in a Sydney prison unit that an independent watchdog previously <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/dec/08/long-bay-jail-nsw-prison-watchdog-report">recommended be closed</a> because it “simply cannot provide a safe environment”.</p> <p>The man was on remand, awaiting a hearing or sentencing, when he took his own life on Sunday in Long Bay correctional centre’s Metropolitan Special Programs Centre (MSPC).</p> <p>Corrective Service NSW said “staff commenced a medical response, but he was pronounced deceased by paramedics on Sunday”.</p> <p>“Corrective Services NSW and NSW police are investigating. Any death in custody is immediately reported to the NSW coroner and subject to a compulsory, rigorous public inquiry,” a spokesperson said.</p> <aside class="element element-rich-link element--thumbnail"> <p> <span>Related: </span><a href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/dec/08/long-bay-jail-nsw-prison-watchdog-report">Prison watchdog urges closure of ‘unsafe’ parts of Long Bay prison</a> </p> </aside>  <p>The NSW prison population has reached a record high. It grew more in the four months to March than in the previous four years, according to Bureau of Crime Statistics and Research data released on Thursday.</p> <p>There were more than 14,000 people in the state’s jails in March.</p> <p>The rise was driven by an increase in the remand population and Indigenous adults in custody. There was a record number of Indigenous people in jail, and people being held on domestic violence offences. The latter cohort made up 28% of the prison population.</p> <p>Last year, there was a <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/oct/15/indigenous-deaths-in-custody-nsw-2025-australia-record-high-coroner">record number of Indigenous deaths</a> in custody.</p>  <a class="element element-link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/email-newsletters?CMP=copyembed&amp;CMP=emailbutton" > Sign up for the Breaking News Australia email </a>  <p>In December, the inspector for custodial services recommended three out of five units at Long Bay – including the MSPC where the 19-year-old died – be permanently closed after it was inspected in 2023 and 2024.</p> <p>The <a href="https://inspectorcustodial.nsw.gov.au/documents/inspection-reports/Inspection_of_the_Long_Bay_Correctional_Complex_2023-24.pdf">report described the rooms in MSPC</a> as being run-down and small, with little natural light or ventilation. It said the accommodation wings were “susceptible to weather conditions and extreme temperatures”.</p> <p>“We observed mouldy walls, rusted furniture and evidence of vermin,” the inspector, Fiona Rafter, wrote. She observed “ligature points in cells across all areas”.</p> <p>“The age and condition of MSPC means it simply cannot provide a safe environment for people in custody, particularly those with needs arising from disability, age and frailty and mental illness,” the report stated. “We have no choice but to recommend its closure.”</p> <p>Corrections provided its formal response to the report in early March. It is not yet publicly available.</p> <p>There <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2026/may/01/nsw-deaths-custody-record-number-ntwnfb">were 39 deaths in custody</a> in NSW in 2025. Of those, 12 were First Nations people, a record high. Twenty-two per cent of the overall deaths were by hanging, despite millions being invested <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/series/fatal-inaction">to remove ligature points</a>.</p> <p>The department has an existing $16m program under way that began in the 2023-24 financial year to remove all ligature points from cells over four years across the correctional system.</p> <p>A <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/jul/11/death-toll-from-suicides-using-known-ligature-points-in-australian-prisons-climbs-on-release-of-new-information-ntwnfb">Guardian Australia investigation found</a> last year that there had been 10 hanging deaths from two ligature points at Long Bay correctional centre between 1995 and 2017. Corrective services said at the time it had now been addressed in “several wings at Long Bay” by the installation of mesh screens.</p> <p>Sue Higginson, the Greens justice spokesperson, said she was “absolutely broken” to hear of Sunday’s death.</p> <p>She moved in the NSW parliament in February for the MSCPC and other units to be closed, but the motion was blocked by the government. She said at the time the decision to keep them open was putting “lives at risk”.</p> <p>“My thoughts are with the family of this young man,” she said on Wednesday.</p> <p>The minister for corrections, Anoulack Chanthivong, declined to comment because an investigation was under way.</p> <p><em>• In Australia, the crisis support service Lifeline is 13 11 14.
