In 2028, Los Angeles might use flying taxis to help people travel during the Olympics. This could change how people get around the city during the big event.

Flying taxis, also known as eVTOL (electric vertical takeoff and landing) vehicles, are small aircraft that can take off and land vertically. They are powered by electricity, which makes them more environmentally friendly than traditional airplanes or helicopters.

The idea is that these flying taxis could help reduce traffic congestion during the Olympics. With thousands of visitors expected in Los Angeles, the roads are likely to be very busy. Flying taxis could offer a faster way to get from one venue to another.

Several companies are working on developing flying taxis. Some of these vehicles can carry up to four passengers and can fly at speeds of up to 200 miles per hour. They are designed to be quiet and safe.

However, there are still some challenges to overcome. The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) needs to approve the use of these vehicles. Pilots will need special training to fly them. And there needs to be places for the flying taxis to take off and land.

Despite these challenges, many people are excited about the possibility of using flying taxis during the Olympics. It could be a glimpse into the future of transportation.

If the plan works, flying taxis could become a regular part of transportation in Los Angeles and other cities around the world. They could help reduce traffic, cut down on pollution, and make travel faster and more convenient.

The 2028 Olympics could be the perfect time to showcase this new technology. It would give people from all over the world a chance to see flying taxis in action and imagine how they could change our lives in the future.
